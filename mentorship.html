Meet our Exceptional Mentors

Caren Grown

Senior Director for Gender at World Bank Group

Caren Grown is senior director for gender at the World Bank. From 2011-2013, she served as senior gender advisor at USAID, where she crafted the Gender Equality and Female Empowerment policy. Her recent books include "The Feminist Economics of Trade and Taxation and Gender Equity."

Email her @ caren.grown@worldbank.org

Loujain al-Hathloul

Activist

Loujain al-Hathloul is a Saudi activist and prominent figure in the women’s rights movement. She played a key role in the campaign that led to the 2018 law allowing women to drive. In 2015, she ran in Saudi Arabia’s first municipal elections open to women and has also advocated for women’s right to travel without a male guardian. Her activism gained global attention after she was imprisoned during a crackdown on dissent.

Email her @ loujain.al-hathloul@gmail.com

Maryam Monsef

Minister of International Development & Women and Gender Equality

Maryam Monsef is Canada's minister for women and gender equality, where she leads the federal government’s efforts to ensure all women, girls and gender non-conforming people have the opportunity to succeed. She is the co-founder of the Red Pashmina Campaign, a grassroots initiative which has raised over CA$150,000 to support women in Afghanistan. She is a former community organiser and co-recipient of the YMCA’s Peace Medallion. She served as minister of democratic institutions from November 2015 to January 2017.

Email her @ maryam.monsef@canada.ca

Phumzile Mlambo-Ngcuka

Executive Director, UN Women Global

Phumzile Mlambo-Ngcuka is the executive director of UN Women. In that role, she has had a strong focus on highlighting the rights and activism of rural women. She also founded the HeforShe campaign, which aims to mobilise a million men and boys to support gender parity. Mlambo-Ngcuka served as deputy president of South Africa from 2005 to 2008, the first woman to hold the position, and was actively involved in the struggle to end apartheid.

Email her @ phumzile.mlambo-ngcuka@unwomen.org
