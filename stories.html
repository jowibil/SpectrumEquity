SUCCESS STORIES AROUND THE WORLD

Explore how workplaces around the world are turning commitment into action by promoting gender-responsive practices and achieving lasting transformation.

Cisco

Championing Diversity and Inclusion

Read More

Challenge: Ensuring workplace equality amidst industry-wide pushbacks on DEI (Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion) initiatives.
Action Taken: Implemented the "proximity initiative" to foster understanding of diverse identities and conducted annual pay parity analyses. Results:

  • Earned the top spot on the FT-Statista list of European Diversity Leaders.
  • Maintained low attrition rates through flexible work policies and diverse talent accelerators.
Testimonial: Cisco's commitment to diversity is driven by the business case for diverse, productive, and innovative teams.

Coca-cola

Empowering Women Leaders

Read More

Challenge: Increasing female representation in senior leadership roles.
Action Taken: Established the Global Women’s Leadership Council and launched the Women in Leadership program to accelerate the development and promotion of women.
Results:

  • Between 2017 and 2020, women in senior leadership roles globally increased from 31.4% to 33.8%.
  • Appointed four new North America Operations Zone Presidents—all qualified women—in 2020.
Testimonial: The company believes that investing in and empowering women benefits both the business and its communities.

Google

Advancing Gender Equality in Tech

Read More

Challenge: Underrepresentation of women in technical and leadership roles, coupled with concerns over pay equity.
Action Taken:

  • Diversity Reporting: Google publishes annual Diversity Annual Reports to track and share progress on diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) initiatives.
  • Pay Equity Analysis: In 2016, Google conducted an analysis across 52 major job categories and reported no gender pay gap.
  • Legal Settlements: In 2022, Google agreed to pay $118 million to settle a class-action gender discrimination lawsuit involving over 15,000 women.
  • Leadership Perspectives: Female leaders at Google have shared insights on promoting gender equality in the workplace, emphasizing the importance of leadership, data, and self-promotion.
Results:
  • Workforce Composition: As of the latest reports, women constitute approximately 30% of Google's global workforce, with
  • Policy Adjustments: Google has made changes to its DEI policies in response to external regulations, including discontinuing certain diversity hiring goals.
Testimonial: "We’ve found that the products that deliver the most value are the ones that bring more opportunity to the people who use them." — Melonie Parker, Chief Diversity Officer, Google
