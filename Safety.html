"Harassment & Workplace Safety"

Addressing Gender-Based Harassment and Ensuring Workplace Safety in the Philippines

Gender-based harassment is a serious problem in many workplaces in the Philippines. It affects mental health, lowers job satisfaction, and can cause employees—especially women—to leave their jobs. A 2023 survey showed that about 1 in 5 Filipino workers have experienced some form of harassment at work, including sexual, physical, or psychological abuse. Sadly, many do not report these incidents because they fear retaliation or believe nothing will change. To help solve this problem, the government has passed laws like the Anti-Sexual Harassment Act (RA 7877) and the Safe Spaces Act (RA 11313). These laws require employers to prevent and respond to harassment by setting up clear rules, offering training, and creating safe ways for employees to report abuse. However, challenges still exist. Many workplaces need to do more to enforce these rules and protect victims. Creating a safe and respectful environment means having clear reporting systems, protecting those who speak up, and making sure every employee feels supported and valued.
