Unconscious Bias in Hiring and Promotion in the Philippines

Unconscious bias continues to affect how people are hired and promoted in the workplace. Even when women and non-binary individuals have the same or better qualifications than their male counterparts, they are often overlooked for job opportunities or leadership roles. These biases may not be intentional, but they are shaped by long-standing beliefs and stereotypes about gender roles and abilities. In the Philippines, women are well-represented in the workforce, but fewer of them make it to top-level positions. For example, while women hold many mid-level jobs, only a small percentage are in executive roles or boardrooms. Some employers may still assume that women are less committed to work because of family responsibilities or that men are more suited for leadershipâ€”even when the evidence says otherwise. To reduce this kind of bias, companies need to review their hiring and promotion processes. This can include using gender-blind resume screening, training managers to recognize their own biases, and setting clear criteria for promotions. Creating fair and inclusive workplaces helps ensure that everyone has an equal chance to grow and succeed, regardless of gender.
